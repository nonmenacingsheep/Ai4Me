What she's called. Changing it updates every mention across the app — and she'll know herself by the new name.
Pick any model whose API key you've set (DeepSeek, OpenAI, OpenRouter, Groq), or a local Ollama model if Ollama is running. Cloud frees your GPU.
Local-model memory window. 4096 keeps a 12GB GPU fully on-GPU with a voice changer. Ignored for DeepSeek.
Route to your voice changer's input (e.g. CABLE Input).
Which mic Aitha listens through. She mutes herself while she's speaking, so she won't hear her own voice.
Stops the mic from hearing Aitha's own voice out of your speakers and transcribing it back to her. Leave on for speakers; turn it off if you wear headphones.
Reveal the raw <note>, <deletenote>, <journal> and <explore> blocks she emits, for debugging.
Control what Aitha does on her own and how often. Turning a drive off stops it entirely; the frequency sets how eagerly she acts when it's on — she still chooses, you just bias it. Lower frequencies (or a slower check-in) mean fewer background LLM calls, which saves tokens on pricier models.
She starts conversations on her own when you're at the keyboard.
She writes private entries to herself (you can read them in Mantle).
She wanders off to research, develop a note, or prep something for you. This is the most token-hungry drive.
How often she even considers acting. Longer = calmer and cheaper; shorter = more responsive and more frequent.
The big look of the chat. Aitha can change this too — and you'll both see it instantly, with each of you knowing who did it.
Nudge a preset to taste. Leave these to let the preset decide.
Everything she remembers about you long-term. Delete any, or add your own.
Aitha keeps the last 24 messages as short-term context. If she gets stuck in a loop or a weird mood, wipe it to give her a clean slate. This won't touch her long-term memories.
